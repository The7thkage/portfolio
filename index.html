Component Designs

Cockpit

Pedal Box Assembly

Dual-pedal brake and throttle assembly designed for FSAE driver accommodation requirements. The assembly integrates dual brake master cylinders for front-rear brake bias control, mounted on a structural base plate with lightening cutouts.

  • Dual master cylinder configuration for front/rear brake bias adjustment
  • Structural base plate with lightening cutouts to reduce mass
  • Designed to accommodate 95th percentile driver reach per FSAE cockpit rules
  • Pedal pivot geometry optimised for consistent pedal feel across stroke
  • Full SolidWorks assembly with hardware modelled (fasteners, clevis pins, rod ends)
Drivetrain / Steering

Steering Assembly

Rack-and-pinion steering system with upper and lower universal joints to accommodate the steering column angle relative to the rack. The custom steering wheel incorporates ergonomic cutouts for driver grip and weight reduction.

  • Rack-and-pinion mechanism for precise, low-friction steering response
  • Double U-joint column to accommodate column angle to rack geometry
  • Custom steering wheel designed for driver ergonomics and minimum mass
  • Column and rack positioned to clear chassis members and driver knee envelope
  • Assembly fully constrained in SolidWorks with mating conditions validated
Structural

Firewall & Structural Panels

The firewall — visible as the vertical panel integrated into the rear of the spaceframe — forms the mandatory separation between the high-voltage accumulator bay and the driver cockpit, as required by FSAE EV rules. Our team designed this panel and the underbody surface to interface correctly with the chassis geometry produced by the chassis department.

  • Firewall designed per FSAE EV rules requiring physical separation of HV accumulator and cockpit zones
  • Mounting interface coordinated with chassis team rollhoop and rear bulkhead geometry
  • Underbody panel designed for ground clearance compliance and aerodynamic underfloor flow
  • Panel geometry modelled to minimise weight while maintaining required rigidity
  • Cross-team integration: our mounting holes aligned to chassis tube positions defined by chassis department
